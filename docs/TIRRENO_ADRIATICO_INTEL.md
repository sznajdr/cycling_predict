Leader jersey: dark blue | Trophy: Golden Trident of Neptune

## RACE OVERVIEW

- **Dates:** Mon 09/03 – Sun 15/03/2026
- **Stages:** 7
- **Total distance:** 1,170+ km
- **Total elevation:** ~15,550 m (notably more than 2025)
- **Start:** Lido di Camaiore (west coast, Tirreno)
- **Finish:** San Benedetto del Tronto (east coast, Adriatic)
- **UCI WorldTour** since 2005 (founded 1966, run every year since)

**2025 edition:** Juan Ayuso (ESP) won GC ahead of Filippo Ganna and Antonio Tiberi. Ganna dominated the opening TT; Jonathan Milan took 2 sprint stages. Ayuso delivered the decisive blow on stage 6 (summit finish).

**Historical GC winners (notable):** Roger de Vlaeminck (record: 6 consecutive wins 1972–77, 15 stage wins total), Francesco Moser, Giuseppe Saronni, Joop Zoetemelk, Tony Rominger, Rolf Sorensen, Cadel Evans, Vincenzo Nibali, Alberto Contador, Nairo Quintana, Fabian Cancellara (2008), Greg Van Avermaet (2016), Primož Roglič, Simon Yates, Tadej Pogačar, Jonas Vingegaard, Juan Ayuso (2025).

## STAGE-BY-STAGE BREAKDOWN

### Stage 1 — Mon 09/03 | ITT: Lido di Camaiore → Lido di Camaiore | **11.5 km**
**Type:** Individual time trial (flat, coastal)
**Start:** 12:40 | **Last rider:** ~15:45

- Completely flat. Starts on Viale Cristoforo Colombo, mostly one straight line.
- 180-degree turnaround at the 4.5 km intermediate point, then back to finish.
- Pure power discipline — no cornering difficulty, no technical elements.
- Identical profile to 2025.
- **Weather:** ~15°C, minimal wind — near-perfect conditions.
- Ganna has won this opening TT 3 of the last 4 editions; Ayuso won the other.

### Stage 2 — Tue 10/03 | Camaiore → San Gimignano | **206 km**
**Type:** Hilly with gravel sectors and uphill finish
**Start:** 10:55

- Small climb immediately from the start, then a long flat/transitional section.
- **Final 80 km:** constant up-and-down, technical descents, positioning crucial — splits possible.
- **Gravel sector:** starts ~60 km from finish; 1.3 km at 7.6% entirely on *sterrato* (gravel), followed by 5+ km of gravel, then <2 km back on tarmac.
- **Final ramp:** 1.2 km at ~7% on asphalt entering San Gimignano.
- **Full finishing climb to San Gimignano:** ~7 km total, with a 15% ramp in the last meters entering the medieval historic center.
- Profile favors puncheurs, gravel riders, and breakaway specialists — not pure climbers.
- **Van Aert and Van der Poel are the two riders most likely to target this stage.** Jan Christen (UAE) and Paul Lapeira (Decathlon AG2R) also suit the profile.
- Note: riders who limit losses in stage 1 TT can take the blue jersey here.

---

### Stage 3 — Wed 11/03 | Cortona → Magliano de' Marsi | **221 km**
**Type:** Sprinters' stage (demanding)
**Start:** 10:40

- Long distance (serves as Milan–Sanremo simulation). Flat start, undulating final two-thirds.
- 2,400 m elevation gain — not extreme but cumulative fatigue matters.
- Last relevant climb ends **40 km from finish:** 7 km at 4%. Teams may try to harden here.
- Long straight finish into Magliano de' Marsi — suited to experienced sprint trains.
- **Sprint contenders:** Jonathan Milan, Paul Magnier, Jasper Philipsen, Arnaud De Lie, Tobias Lund Andresen, Pavel Bittner, Danny van Poppel, Kaden Groves.

---

### Stage 4 — Thu 12/03 | Tagliacozzo → Martinsicuro | **213 km**
**Type:** Chaotic/punchy — designed for chaos
**Start:** 10:25

- Departs from altitude in Tagliacozzo, net descent of ~700 m to the coast finish — but with 2,800 m climbing along the way.
- **Opening two climbs:** ~12 km at ~5% — enough to forge a powerful break with climbers and GC riders.
- **Four decisive climbs in the second half:**
  - 7.6 km at 4.5% (49 km to go)
  - 3.1 km at 3.8% (28 km)
  - 1.2 km at 5.3% (19 km)
  - **1.5 km at 8.4%** (ends 12 km to go) — key moment
- Mostly flat run into Martinsicuro after the last climb — tactical attacks possible.
- Stage opens well for baroudeurs and breakaway specialists.

---

### Stage 5 — Fri 13/03 | Marotta-Mondolfo → Mombaroccio | **184 km**
**Type:** Explosive/punchy — classics-style hills throughout
**Start:** 10:55

- 3,800 m elevation gain. Climbs from kilometer 1 to the finish.
- **Monte delle Ceane:** 7+ km at 7%+ average — but ends **80 km from the finish**; too early to decide alone.
- Steady stream of punchy hills throughout — very high average difficulty.
- **Final climb: Santuario del Beato Sante** — 4.2 km slightly above 6%.
- Last climb ends only **1.5 km from finish**, followed by descent to Mombaroccio at the line.
- Very open tactically: breakaways viable or GC duel on the final climb. Team strength and race dynamics will decide.

---

### Stage 6 — Sat 14/03 | San Severino Marche → Camerino | **188 km** ⭐ QUEEN STAGE
**Type:** GC decider — pure climbers and puncheurs
**Start:** 10:45

- 4,000+ m elevation gain. Rare profile even by Apennines standards.
- Opens with explosive climb from San Severino (ideal launchpad for break).
- **Sassoletto climb:** 13 km at 7% average — golden opportunity for climbers early.
- **Circuit format:** 4 laps of a loop including the Camerino wall each time.
  - Riders see the finish climb at 85, 59, 30, and 0 km to go.
  - **Wall of Madonna delle Carceri (Camerino):** 3.2 km at 9%, with ramps of **18% and 20%**.
  - Final straight on cobblestones.
- Each lap of the circuit allows tactical team play and numerical superiority.
- Expected key riders here: Isaac del Toro, Matteo Jorgenson, Primož Roglič, Richard Carapaz, Felix Gall, Ilan Van Wilder, Antonio Tiberi.

---

### Stage 7 — Sun 15/03 | Civitanova Marche → San Benedetto del Tronto | **142 km**
**Type:** Sprint finale (traditional closing stage)
**Start:** 12:30

- Short stage (142 km) with an explosive start — two short climbs for late attackers.
- Final hours completely flat and very fast.
- Finish on Via Buozzi in San Benedetto del Tronto — not technical, suited to mass sprint.
- Breakaway can be dangerous given the fast flat roads.
- Jonathan Milan won here in 2025.

## TACTICAL NOTES

- **Stage 2 gravel is the key differentiator in the first half.** A strong performance here by van Aert or van der Poel could create an early blue jersey and force GC riders to react.
- **Ganna's TT advantage** on stage 1 may not translate to GC dominance (as in 2025 where Ayuso eventually overturned it on stage 6), but it sets a psychological and tactical benchmark.
- **BORA's three-leader strategy** (Roglic + Hindley + Pellizzari) makes them the most numerically dominant GC squad. Expect them to use this tactically, especially on stages 4–6.
- **Stage 6 is decided on the Camerino wall** — 18–20% ramps on cobbles with 4 passes through the circuit. Del Toro's explosiveness is the main weapon there.
- **Stage 5 (Mombaroccio)** could spring a surprise if GC teams over-commit to Camerino; open stage for a bold move.
- **Tirreno has historically been used as Milan–Sanremo prep.** Van der Poel targeted it explicitly in 2025 before winning La Primavera. Expect similar motivation in 2026.
